The real problem

You don't have a content problem. You have a pitch problem.

Three reasons most aspiring UGC creators stay stuck at platform gigs.

01

Platforms pay the floor.

Insense, Cohley, JoinBrands exist to pay you the least the market will tolerate. Their entire business depends on your willingness to accept $30 for a 3-video deliverable.

02

Your DMs are one-liners.

"Hey, love your brand! Would you be open to working together?" That's a form letter a brand's intern deletes in three seconds. You need a shot list, a price, and a delivery date.

03

You don't have a lane.

Generic UGC competes with every creator on earth. A niche pulls brands toward you instead of you chasing them. Almost nobody teaches this step first.

The Method

The pitch every paid UGC creator sends. Three parts.

I don't teach "post more." I teach the exact three-part pitch that turned my reply rate from 2% to 20% and started landing four-figure deals.

  • i.
    A shot list, not a pitch. Brands want to see what you're going to make before they pay. One-line descriptions of five pieces of content. That's the whole first paragraph.
  • ii.
    A price with an anchor. Never "what's your budget." Say "$500 for three pieces, $800 for five." The anchor does the work. The brand picks one. You get paid more than you would have asked for.
  • iii.
    A delivery date, specific. "First round shot by Friday." Specificity tells them you've done this before. It closes the sale without asking for it.
