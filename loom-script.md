# Evergreen Loom Script — Zytrom D100 Walkthrough

A reusable 2-3 minute Loom script for walking prospects through their hub page. Record once, reuse across similar UGC / creator-economy prospects by swapping the two spoken placeholders at the start and mid-point.

**Spoken placeholders (say live, don't pre-record):**
- `[NAME]` → prospect's first name
- `[SPECIFIC GAP]` → the one gap you most want them to feel (landing page, funnel, community, etc.)

**Target runtime:** 2:00 - 2:45
**Screen setup:** Open the hub page ([https://henryqueroa.github.io/thesilveryouth-package/](https://henryqueroa.github.io/thesilveryouth-package/)) before recording. Cursor ready on the first accordion.

---

## 0:00 - 0:20 · Opening (say live, camera bubble on)

> "Hey [NAME], this is Henryque. I run a small marketing studio called Zytrom and I put something together specifically for your business that I think you're going to want to see."

> "No pitch in the first twenty seconds, I promise. I just want to walk you through what I built."

**Delivery note:** Lean in slightly. Smile. Keep the camera bubble in the corner. Your face on the hook matters more than the words.

---

## 0:20 - 0:50 · The overview (screen share, scroll the header)

> "So what you're looking at is a package I built exclusively for you. There's research, five campaign angles, three ad script concepts, a live landing page in your brand, and a VSL outline."

> "Everything is yours. No signup, no form, no catch. I built it because the fastest way to show you what I see is to just build the thing."

**Cursor action:** Slowly scroll from header past the stats bar. Pause on "Built Exclusively For [Prospect Name]." Let them see their own name on the page.

---

## 0:50 - 1:35 · The insight (expand one accordion, talk over it)

> "I want to show you the piece I think matters most. [SPECIFIC GAP]."

> *(Click open the Research Snapshot accordion. Point to the gap list.)*

> "Right now your funnel is basically 'social content, then Stan Store checkout.' That works for the people who are already sold. But it means everyone who isn't ready to buy today is leaving and never coming back."

> "The landing page I built (I'll show you in a second) is the piece that captures all those people. It tells the full story, it has the social proof slots, and there's an email opt-in for the ones who want to think about it."

**Delivery note:** This is the insight moment. Slow down. Let the point land. Don't rush into the next thing.

---

## 1:35 - 2:10 · The landing page reveal (click through to live page)

> *(Click "View The Live Landing Page" in the accordion. Landing page loads.)*

> "This is live right now. It's in your brand colors. It's mobile responsive. You could point traffic at it today."

> "I'll let you explore it on your own. The main sections you'll care about are the Method section, which is basically your three-part pitch turned into your sales argument, and the FAQ at the bottom where I tried to answer the real objections your audience has."

**Cursor action:** Quick scroll-through of the landing page. Don't linger. Just enough for them to register that it's real.

---

## 2:10 - 2:40 · The close (back to camera bubble, casual)

> "Everything you've seen is yours to keep whether we ever talk or not. Take the landing page, strip out my name, push it live. I'd be happy to know it helped."

> "If you want to talk about implementing this or building the rest of it out together, my calendar is in the email I sent. No pitch deck, no pressure, just a conversation."

> "Appreciate you taking the time to watch. Have a good one."

**Delivery note:** Wrap on a smile. Do not end on a CTA ask. The CTA is in the email. The Loom is value.

---

## Filming and posting checklist

Before you record:
- [ ] Hub page loaded, cursor in position
- [ ] Landing page loaded in second tab (so the click-through is instant)
- [ ] Camera bubble positioned (bottom-right is standard)
- [ ] Mic check (AirPods or USB mic, not laptop built-in)
- [ ] Single clean background (no notification popups)

After you record:
- [ ] Watch your own playback once at 1.5x. Cut if it's over 3:00.
- [ ] Name the Loom "Built for [BUSINESS NAME]" so it looks personalized in the inbox preview
- [ ] Generate thumbnail with their brand name visible
- [ ] Paste the Loom link as the first line of Email 1 (before the hub page link)

## Why this script is evergreen

The only live-spoken placeholders are `[NAME]` (hook) and `[SPECIFIC GAP]` (insight moment). Everything else is structural. You can cut one 40-second clip of the overview, one 50-second clip of the landing page reveal, and one 30-second close. Record the two personalized pieces fresh each time, stitch with the evergreen clips, and you have a 10-minute turnaround per Loom instead of 45.

Most D100 creators ignore this. They either:
1. Record every Loom from scratch (takes too long, quality drops by prospect #15)
2. Send the same fully-recorded Loom to every prospect (prospect notices immediately, kills trust)

The hybrid approach wins on both axes.
